About KCLES

King’s College London has one of the oldest engineering programmes in the UK. The Department of Civil Engineering and Mining was established in 1838. Following that, in 1847, the King’s College London Engineering Society was founded [1]. Popularity of the society gained traction in the 1870s, and since then, the society has maintained a presence at King’s. Through the Second World War, the society

The society’s early activities revolved around regular readings of scientific and engineering papers, such as early photography, manufacturing methods, weapons development, and infrastructure-related projects. In the present, we are more focused on speaker events, society socials, and hands-on workshops on any engineering topic.
